Jehovah's Witnesses are a Christian cult founded in the 1870s in the United States, known for their distinct beliefs and practices. They emphasize the use of God's name as "Jehovah," reject the Trinity doctrine, and believe in a literal 144,000 who will go to heaven while others live eternally on a paradise Earth. They are known for their door-to-door evangelism and refusal of certain societal norms, such as military service, blood transfusions, and celebration of holidays.
